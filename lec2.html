Elimination

1x+2y+z=23x+8y+z=120x+4y+z=2\begin{align} 1x + 2y + z &= 2 \\ 3x + 8y + z &= 12 \\ 0x + 4y + z &= 2 \end{align}

Start with the first “pivot”, the goal is to multiply the number and subtract from the below number(s) to turn it/them into 0. Here we multiply by 3 and subtract. Row 3, Col 1 is ignored since it’s already 0.

[1_21381041][2122]\begin{bmatrix} \underline{1} & 2 & 1 \\ 3 & 8 & 1 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \qquad \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 12 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}

Do the same with the second “pivot”.

[1_2102_−2041][262]\begin{bmatrix} \underline{1} & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & \underline{2} & -2 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \qquad \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 6 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}

And the third “pivot”.

[1_2102_−2005_][2610]\begin{bmatrix} \underline{1} & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & \underline{2} & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & \underline{5} \end{bmatrix} \qquad \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 6 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}
  • The above 3x3 matrix is known as U and the 3x1 vector is b
  • Rows can be swapped to avoid zero pivots which are not allowed

From here it’s possible to solve for x, y and z. This is known as Back Substitution.

1x+2y+1z=20x+2y−2z=120x+0y+5z=2x=2y=1z=−2 \begin{align} 1x + 2y + 1z &= 2 \\ 0x + 2y - 2z &= 12 \\ 0x + 0y + 5z &= 2 \end{align} \qquad \begin{align} x = 2 \\ y = 1 \\ z = -2 \end{align}
